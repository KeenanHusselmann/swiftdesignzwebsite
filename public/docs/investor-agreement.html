01 — Nature of Agreement
  • Swift Designz is not a registered company. It is a freelance business operated by Keenan Husselmann in his personal capacity.
  • This Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, or registered entity. The term "shares" or "stake" as used herein refers to a contractual profit-sharing entitlement, not legal equity in a registered company.
  • This Agreement grants the Investor a contractual right to receive annual dividend payouts based on their stake percentage, and the right to sell that stake after the lock-in period.
  • The Business Owner retains full and sole control over all business operations, decisions, pricing, clients, and strategic direction.
  • Should Swift Designz become a registered company (Pty Ltd, CC, or similar) during the term of this Agreement, both parties agree to convert the Investor's contractual stake into equivalent legal shares in the registered entity.
02 — Investment Structure
  • The Investor shall make an initial capital contribution of N$3,000.00 (Three Thousand Namibian Dollars) upon signing this Agreement.
  • Thereafter, the Investor shall contribute N$2,000.00 (Two Thousand Namibian Dollars) per month for a continuous period of 5 (five) years (60 months), commencing from the month following the initial contribution.
  • All payments shall be made via EFT to the Business Owner's bank account (details below) by the 5th of each calendar month.
  • If a monthly contribution is missed, the Investor shall make it up within 30 days. If 3 or more consecutive contributions are missed, the Business Owner may freeze the stake at its current level until contributions resume.
  • The investment amount is not a loan and is not guaranteed to be repaid. Returns are dependent on business performance.
  • Both parties shall keep a shared record of all contributions made, with dates and amounts, signed or acknowledged by both parties at least quarterly.

04 — Annual Dividend Payouts
  • The Investor shall receive no monthly returns. All returns are paid exclusively as annual dividends.
  • "Annual Dividend" is calculated as: the Investor's current stake percentage × the annual net profit of Swift Designz for that financial year.
  • "Net Profit" is defined as: total revenue received by Swift Designz in a financial year (January to December), minus all legitimate business expenses. Business expenses include but are not limited to: hosting fees, domain fees, software subscriptions, marketing costs, subcontractor payments, transaction fees, equipment, and any other costs directly related to service delivery.
  • Dividends shall be calculated after the close of each financial year and paid within 60 days of the year-end (i.e., by the end of February each year).
  • If net profit for a given year is zero or negative, no dividend is owed. Losses do not carry forward or accumulate as debt.
  • Payment shall be made via EFT to the Investor's nominated bank account.
  • The Business Owner shall provide the Investor with an annual financial summary alongside each dividend payment, showing: total revenue, total expenses, net profit, Investor's stake %, and dividend amount.

05 — Lock-In Period & Stake Sale
  • The Investor's stake is locked in for 5 (five) years from the date of the initial investment. During this period, the Investor may not sell, transfer, or assign their stake to any third party.
  • After the 5-year lock-in period has elapsed, the Investor is free to sell their stake, subject to the following conditions:
    • The Business Owner has the right of first refusal — the Investor must first offer the stake to the Business Owner before offering it to any third party.
    • The Business Owner shall have 60 days from the date of the offer to accept or decline.
    • If the Business Owner declines, the Investor may sell to a third party, provided the Business Owner approves the buyer in writing (approval not to be unreasonably withheld).
  • The sale price of the stake shall be determined by mutual agreement at the time of sale, taking into account the current business valuation, annual revenue, and cumulative contributions made.
  • Upon sale of the stake, the buyer assumes the Investor's rights under this Agreement, and a new agreement or addendum shall be signed by all parties.
06 — Early Termination of Contributions
  • If the Investor wishes to stop monthly contributions before the 5-year period ends, they may do so with 60 days' written notice.
  • In this case, the Investor's stake shall be frozen at the level corresponding to their total cumulative contributions as per the Share Value Schedule in Clause 03.
  • The Investor shall continue to receive annual dividends based on their frozen stake.
  • The frozen stake remains subject to the 5-year lock-in from the original start date — early termination of contributions does not shorten the lock-in period.
  • If contributions are stopped, the Investor cannot resume contributions under this Agreement unless both parties agree in writing to an addendum.
07 — Financial Reporting & Transparency
  • The Business Owner shall provide the Investor with an annual financial summary within 60 days of year-end, showing: total revenue, total expenses, net profit, and the Investor's dividend calculation.
  • The Business Owner shall also provide a quarterly contribution statement confirming all monthly contributions received and the Investor's current cumulative total and stake percentage.
  • The Investor may request additional detail on specific expense items, which the Business Owner shall provide in reasonable time.
  • Both parties agree to operate in good faith and transparency regarding all financial matters.
08 — Investor Role & Limitations
  • The Investor is a passive/silent investor and shall have no authority over business operations, client relationships, pricing, hiring, or strategic decisions.
  • The Investor may offer advice or suggestions, but the Business Owner is under no obligation to act upon them.
  • The Investor shall not represent themselves as a partner, owner, or agent of Swift Designz to any third party.
  • The Investor shall not enter into agreements, incur debts, or make commitments on behalf of Swift Designz.
09 — Buyback Option
  • The Business Owner reserves the right to buy back the Investor's stake at any time (including during the lock-in period), subject to mutual agreement on price.
  • The minimum buyback value shall be the total cumulative contributions made by the Investor at the time of buyback, minus any dividends already paid.
  • Upon completion of a buyback, this Agreement shall terminate immediately and the Investor shall have no further claim to dividends or the business.
  • The Investor may also request a buyback from the Business Owner, but the Business Owner is under no obligation to accept during the lock-in period.
10 — Risk Acknowledgement
  • The Investor acknowledges that all business ventures carry inherent risk and that returns are not guaranteed.
  • The Investor's total liability is limited to the cumulative contributions made. The Investor shall not be held liable for any debts, obligations, or losses incurred by Swift Designz beyond this amount.
  • The Business Owner shall not be held personally liable to the Investor for business losses, reduced profits, or periods of no revenue.
  • The Investor confirms that all funds contributed are legitimately obtained and that this investment is made voluntarily and without coercion.
11 — Confidentiality
  • Both parties agree to keep the financial details of this Agreement, including revenue figures, profit amounts, stake percentages, and any business information shared under this Agreement, strictly confidential.
  • Neither party shall disclose confidential information to any third party without the written consent of the other party, unless required by law.
  • This obligation survives the termination of this Agreement for a period of 2 (two) years.
12 — Dispute Resolution
  • Both parties agree to resolve any disputes arising from this Agreement through good-faith negotiation in the first instance.
  • If a dispute cannot be resolved amicably within 30 days, either party may refer the matter to mediation by a mutually agreed mediator.
  • This Agreement shall be governed by and interpreted in accordance with the laws of the Republic of Namibia.
13 — General Provisions
  • This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter herein and supersedes any prior discussions, representations, or agreements.
  • Any amendments to this Agreement must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
  • If any clause is found to be unenforceable, the remaining clauses shall remain in full force and effect.
  • This Agreement is not transferable or assignable by the Investor during the lock-in period. After the lock-in period, transfer is subject to the conditions in Clause 05.
